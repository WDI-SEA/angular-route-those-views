Facts

  • Hippopotamidae suggests that hippos and whales shared a common semiaquatic ancestor
  • Dwarf species evolved on several Mediterranean islands, including Crete (H. creutzburgi), Cyprus (H. minor), Malta (H. melitensis), and Sicily (H. pentlandi)
    • Hippos can be found in both savannah and forest areas.[1] Proper habitat requires enough water to submerge in and grass nearby.
  • Hippopotamus teeth sharpen themselves as they grind together.
  • Their skin secretes a natural sunscreen substance which is red-coloured. The secretion is sometimes referred to as "blood sweat", but is neither blood nor sweat
